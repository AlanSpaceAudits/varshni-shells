Test: do the shells survive a change of observer?

Quasar positions are fixed in space. The yellow marker is the observer. For each group, we draw a sphere around the observer at radius = mean distance from observer to that group's members. Each quasar is recolored by how far it sits from its group's would-be shell.

At Earth (origin), shells fit perfectly (everything green). Move the observer with the sliders and the shells stop fitting — exactly what Varshni claimed.

Varshni 1976, possibility (3): "These shells would disappear if viewed from another galaxy or a quasar."
